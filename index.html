Presentation / Resume

My name is Thomas Johannessen, I'm born 1981, originally from Gildeskål up north in Norway, I live with my girlfriend and our two fantastic daughters (aged 4.5 and 1 years old) in Etterstad, Oslo. My friends say I'm creative, funny and smart. And I think my employers would say that I'm trustworthy, loyal and hard-working. My interests outside work and family is quite complex board games (not Monopoly or Risk. If you know names like Martin Wallace, Vital Lacerda, Reiner Knizia and Matt Eklund we can talk about it later, if not I'll spare you the details) and working out. I'm also interested in cooking, history and keeping up to date with current events. For example I followed a 25 hour Yale univeristy video course on Ukrainian history last year.

And: I want to become a developer (click this paragraph to proceed to relevant section if you want to be a bit efficient with your day. And let's be honest - who's got the time to read through a life story these days?)

Actually I've tried this path before - barely. I studied programming more than 20 years ago, back when "tablets" were actual stone tablets, but dropped out during the second semester to work. I instead studied physiotherapy, which I completed and got my degree in 2006. However, I never actually got to work as a physio, instead I opted into the world of easier money (I thought at the time, it didn't turn out to be quite that simple, although I'we done well over the years) working as a Personal Trainer in Sandvika, Bærum.

This all coincided with me pushing for a career in sports, I had trained full contact kickboxing a few years and eventually found Brazilian Jiu Jitsu back in 2003 in Trondheim during my studies. I've always done sports - football, cross country skiing, swimming and weight lifting, but never really been any good at it. I found that I had some talent for Brazilian Jiu Jitsu (from hereon BJJ, which I realize is a bit of an unfortunate abbreviation). Moving to Oslo I started training at Frontline Academy, the leading academy in Norway for MMA, BJJ and Muay Thai. Working as a PT was a perfect combination for years of hard training, travelling and competing, so I stuck with my work at what was then called Elixia, now SATS.

I eventually graduated to black belt after winning consecutive medals at brown belt in the European Championship as well as Pan-American Open in 2013, Rome Open and Swedish Open. As a black belt I won Nordic Open, Swedish Open, Rome Open, 2x London Open and double gold in Nice Open. I also instructed quite a bit, and travelled around doing seminars. Unfortunately I've been forced to retire from competition after a couple of bad neck and knee injuries with surgeries (nothing that bothers me daily, it's just not compatible with full contact competitive martial arts). Nowadays I still work out almost every day, but more weight lifting, running and bike riding (yup I'm one of them, sorry not sorry). The last few years I've also settled down and started a family, we had our first daughter back in 2018, which is a fantastic but different sort of challenge.

Now we arrive at why you're (maybe, hopefully, I don't know) still reading this: Challenge is the keyword. I need a new one. The last few years I've in addition to my job as a Personal Trainer also had an administrative part time position at Frontline Academy (we're two people sharing the manager position, and been doing that 4 years), during the pandemic both my employers had to shut down shop more or less the whole time. During that time I was working for Oslo Kommune with COVID-19 vaccination, due to my background from physiotherapy. I realize that working more than 15 years for Elixia/SATS has become a bit stale for me, and if I'm going to do something else - now is the time, we're done producing kids and I'm motivated to do something else.

So I started coding, back in mid December 2022. And stuck with it, a couple hours a day since then. Every. single. day. I started out with some Python, but since I have many friends who are real full time professional developers I soon started to get helpful tips. This lead me into JavScript, CSS and HTML in January. I've done the big Jonas Schmedtmann Udemy-course (The Complete JavaScript Course: From Zero to Expert!) as well as many small projects on my own, the Wes Bos "30 JavaScript challenges in 30 days" course etc. One tip I took to heart was to not get stuck in "tutorial hell", so I've always tried to have a small code project going on side of the course, also always trying to come up with my own solution to the problem before watching the tutorial. So far I've focused on modern vanilla JavaScript in tandem with HTML and CSS. Do I think I'm ready to become a fulltime coder already? Absolutely not, as a matter of fact I think you'd be completely mad to hire me. However, I've sent some projects to my developer friends who say that I absolutely should start applying for junior developer positions already. So that's why I do this. At first I thought they were just being nice, but they insist that they're serious. I have good people skills, I pick up on things pretty easily, I've also got a black belt in googling, I'm motivated and I think I can become good at this with some guidance and the right employer.

Edit September 2023: Done several more vanilla JS projects, now doing the big Jonas Schmedtmann React course on Udemy (and of course side projects to practice). Now I'm thinking you'll be slightly less mad to hire me. The rest of the text still applies.
